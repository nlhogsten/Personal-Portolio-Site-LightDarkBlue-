Lunar New Year Dragon Art

These dragons were made for the Fantasy exhibition at the Art at Marks February 2024 show in Honolulu, Hawai‘i. They are meant to commemorate the beginning of the Lunar New Year - an embracement of the revolution and change brought by the Year of the Dragon. All of the dragons paintings were depcited with acrylic paint. The fire and ice dragon face each other and each one consists of ten 8" x 8" canvases with custom stretcher bars and stretched canvas. The last dragon is shown filling it's cup at the foot of a Mauna Loa inspired mountain and is embedded in a floating frame.

Lunar New Year Parade in Chinatown

The show's opening was held on first friday February 2, 2024, the same day as the Lunar New Year parade in Honolulu's Chinatown. As patrons browsed through the gallery the volume of foot traffic grew in the streets as the night deepened. The constant drone of the tanggu drums and clashing of the gongs and cymbals bounced throughout the towering city heights. Crowds gathered around the dozens of chinese dance troupes who went from store to store performing Lion Dances in dragon robes. Families and children enjoyed the sights, took many photos, and fed the dragons with their cash. Sudden thunderous sounds of firecracker fireworks ripped through the streets, marking another establishment blessed with good luck and fortune in the coming year.

It was the perfect night and an honor to display these dragon paintings on this day.
